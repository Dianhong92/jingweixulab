Research

We aim to investigate the ultra-structural reorganization that the occurs when cells transition into or out of dormant/quiescent state. A particular goal will be focused on the in-depth characterization of different metabolic enzyme polymers across diverse organisms spanning the tree of life.

Background

Many proteins are capable to form higher-order assemblies. Apart from cytoskeletal elements, several metabolic enzymes have been found to form elaborated filamentous polymers. Interestingly, these metabolic enzyme polymers are often related to the ultra-structural reorganization upon cell stress. The mechanistic insights and cellular functions underlying the remodeling of these filamentous assemblies, however, are still not well understood.

Research projects

Our lab mainly focus on:

1. Exploring the remodeling of metabolic enzyme polymers in budding yeasts upon starvation

2. Investigating the ultrastructural remodeling of quiescent cells when they transition into the activated state

3. Developing pipelines that complement “Visual proteomics” with other techniques for in-depth characterization of diverse cellular filaments
